WELCOME TO THE SOLAR SYSTEM

The Solar System is the gravitationally bound system of the Sun and all objects that orbit it, including planets, dwarf planets, moons, comets, and asteroids. It formed approximately 4.6 billion years ago from the gravitational collapse of a dense region within a molecular cloud, which gave rise to the Sun and a surrounding protoplanetary disc. The Sun, a G-type main-sequence star, is the central and most massive object in the Solar System, containing over 99.86% of its mass. It generates energy through the fusion of hydrogen into helium in its core. The Solar System consists of eight planets divided into two groups. The inner terrestrial planets—Mercury, Venus, Earth, and Mars—have solid, rocky surfaces. Beyond them lie the gas giants, Jupiter and Saturn, and the ice giants, Uranus and Neptune. These outer planets are composed primarily of gases and liquids, lacking a definite surface. Jupiter and Saturn alone contain nearly 90% of the mass outside the Sun. In addition to the planets, the Solar System includes at least nine recognized dwarf planets, such as Pluto, Eris, and Haumea, along with numerous smaller bodies like asteroids, comets, and meteoroids. Many of these objects are concentrated in specific regions, including the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter and the Kuiper belt beyond Neptune. Further out, the hypothesized Oort cloud contains icy bodies that are the source of long-period comets. The Sun emits a stream of charged particles called the solar wind, creating a protective bubble known as the heliosphere. This extends to the heliopause, the boundary between the Solar System and interstellar space, located roughly 75–90 astronomical units from the Sun. Beyond this lies interstellar space, while the nearest star, Proxima Centauri, is about 4.25 light-years away. All these components exist within the vast Milky Way galaxy.

Solar System Planets !

  • Mercury: The smallest planet in our solar system and closest to the Sun. It has no atmosphere.
  • Venus: Known as Earth's twin due to its similar size. It has a thick, toxic atmosphere.
  • Earth: The only planet known to support life, with a diverse climate and water on its surface.
  • Mars: Known as the Red Planet due to its iron oxide-rich surface. It has the largest volcano in the solar system.
  • Jupiter: The largest planet in the solar system, known for its Great Red Spot and many moons.
  • Saturn: Famous for its stunning ring system made of ice and rock.
  • Uranus: An ice giant with a unique sideways rotation. Its atmosphere contains methane, giving it a blue-green color.
  • Neptune: The farthest planet from the Sun. It has strong winds and storms, and a deep blue color.
  • Pluto (Dwarf Planet): Once considered the ninth planet, now classified as a dwarf planet in the Kuiper Belt.

Space Organisations !

  • ISRO
    The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) is India's national space agency responsible for space exploration, satellite development, and launch operations. Founded in 1962, ISRO has achieved significant milestones, including launching satellites, developing launch vehicles, and conducting lunar and Martian missions. Its programs contribute to India's socio-economic development through applications in various sectors like telecommunications, remote sensing, and disaster management.

  • NASA
    NASA, the US space agency, is responsible for civilian space programs, aeronautics research, and space exploration. Founded in 1958, it has led numerous missions, including the Apollo Moon landings and the Space Shuttle program. Currently, NASA focuses on the ISS, Artemis program, and scientific research in areas like Earth observation, heliophysics, and astrophysics.
